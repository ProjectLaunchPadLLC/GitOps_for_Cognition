A Strided Tensor Descriptor for logical tensor views

A deterministic substrate enabling higher‑order cognitive protocols:

Recursive Meta‑Cognitive Evaluation (RMCE)

Recursive Autopoietic Evolution (RAE)

The paper positions HAML as the foundational layer for deterministic, autopoietic AI systems.

Overall Evaluation
The manuscript is conceptually strong, architecturally ambitious, and philosophically distinctive. It proposes a systems‑level rethinking of AI execution that is rare in contemporary ML research. The emphasis on determinism, memory locality, and hardware‑proximal computation is timely and relevant to the emerging intersection of HPC and AI systems design.

The ideas are novel and compelling, but the paper requires additional rigor to reach its full potential.

1. Clear Identification of the “Abstraction Tax”

Zero pointer indirection in tensor indexing

Logical tensor transformations via stride manipulation

These are well‑motivated and technically sound.

Reduce TTFT

The paper would be significantly strengthened by:

Microbenchmarks comparing Arena allocation vs. malloc
